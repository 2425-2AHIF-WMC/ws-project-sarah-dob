Exploring New York City

Summer 2022

15. - 17. August

I arrived in New York City on the evening of the 15th, leaving the actual sightseeing for the following day. On the 16th, I began exploring the city with a visit to the iconic Grand Central Station, marveling at its grand architecture, the celestial ceiling, and the bustling energy of commuters and tourists. From there, I headed to the Rockefeller Center, where I admired the famous ice-skating rink and the towering Christmas tree, which added a festive charm to the experience. I also wandered around the plaza, soaking in the lively atmosphere and taking in the impressive Art Deco buildings.

On the 17th, I ventured downtown to experience the lively buzz of Wall Street, surrounded by the towering skyscrapers that symbolize the financial hub of the world. The Charging Bull statue was a popular spot, buzzing with tourists snapping photos. Later, I spent a solemn and reflective moment at Ground Zero, where the memorial fountains and museum provided a poignant reminder of the resilience and strength of the city. To round out the day, I enjoyed a peaceful, scenic walk across the Brooklyn Bridge, marveling at its intricate design while taking in stunning views of the Manhattan skyline and the shimmering East River.
18. - 19. August

On the 18th, I spent the entire day at Six Flags in New Jersey, and it was an absolute thrill from start to finish. The park’s massive roller coasters and adrenaline-pumping rides kept me on edge all day, and the lively atmosphere added to the excitement. From the heart-racing drops of Kingda Ka to the loops of Nitro, it was a day full of fun, laughter, and unforgettable memories. By the end of the day, I was happily exhausted, but the energy of the trip kept me going.

The 19th was all about exploring the city and indulging in some retail therapy. I wandered through bustling shopping streets, discovering unique stores and picking up a few souvenirs to remember the trip by. Later, I made my way to Central Park, where I took a peaceful stroll, surrounded by the beauty of nature amidst the city’s chaos. It was a perfect moment of calm in an otherwise action-packed trip. The highlight of the day, though, was my first-ever concert, an experience I’ll never forget. The music, the crowd’s energy, and the electric atmosphere were incredible, making it a night to remember and a perfect way to end such a dynamic day.
20. - 23. August

On the 20th, I explored the vibrant streets of Chinatown in New York City, indulging in a culinary adventure filled with authentic dishes like flavorful dumplings, hand-pulled noodles, and sweet pastries, all surrounded by the bustling energy of the neighborhood. The next day, on the 21st, I shifted to exploring some of the city’s iconic landmarks, starting with the sleek and luxurious Trump Tower. From there, I visited the striking glass-structured Apple Store, marveling at its modern design and innovative displays. I ended the day at the Empire State Building, where the breathtaking panoramic views from the observation deck offered a stunning perspective of the sprawling NYC skyline. On the 22nd, I spent a relaxed day exploring the city, trying a variety of local eats from food trucks and small cafés while soaking in the everyday rhythm of New York. It was a day of simple joys and casual discoveries. On the 23rd, I wrapped up my adventures with a visit to the Intrepid Sea, Air & Space Museum. Exploring the historic aircraft carrier was fascinating, as I learned about its role in naval history and got up close to aircraft and even a space shuttle. It was a captivating experience, offering a glimpse into history and innovation. The museum visit was the perfect way to close out an incredible trip, leaving me with even more memories of NYC's rich and diverse offerings.

24. - 26. August

On the 24th, I took the Staten Island Ferry, which offered breathtaking views of the Statue of Liberty and the Manhattan skyline, a perfect way to enjoy the city's waterfront. Once on Staten Island, I explored a bit before heading to The Vessel, an architectural marvel in Hudson Yards. Climbing its spiral staircase, I was treated to stunning panoramic views of the city, a truly unique experience. The next day, on the 25th, I made my way to Coney Island, where the nostalgic boardwalk, classic amusement rides, and the iconic Nathan's Hot Dogs brought back a sense of summer fun. I also visited the Ghostbusters Firehouse, a fun and quirky stop for movie fans, adding a bit of cinematic magic to my day.

On the 26th, I had one last stroll through the dazzling lights and buzzing crowds of Times Square, soaking in the vibrant energy and reflecting on everything I'd experienced. It was a bittersweet moment, knowing my unforgettable New York City adventure was coming to an end. With a heart full of memories and a camera full of photos, I headed home, already looking forward to the next visit.
